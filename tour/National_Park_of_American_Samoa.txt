history edit the volcanic islands of samoa that dominate the acreage of the national park are composed of  shield volcanoes hot spot pacific plate pliocene holocene the islands are not made up of individual volcanoes, but are rather composed of overlapping and superimposed shield volcanoes built by  basalt lava breccia dikes pleistocene trachyte plugs tuff escarpments while the samoan islands have not shown evidence of volcanism for many years, the hot spot beneath the islands continues to give indications of activity, with a submarine eruption detected just east of american samoa in 1973. the vailulu'u seamount, located east of ta'u, is a future samoan island developing from submarine lava flows, continuing the eastward progress of volcanic development from the hotspot below the islands. the lava flows forming the  seamount radiometric evidence exists of past submarine and surface  landslides weathering erosion mass wasting fiji 12 threats edit the coral reefs are under
