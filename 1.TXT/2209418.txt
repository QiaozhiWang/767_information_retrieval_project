and celine, the french-american couple who once met on a train in vienna. they now live in paris with twin daughters, but have spent a summer in greece on the invitation of an author colleague of jesse's. when the vacation is over and jesse must send his teenage son off to the states, he begins to question his life decisions, and his relationship with celine is at risk.        written by  peter brandt nielsen writer jesse wallace is married with céline and they have twin daughters, ella and nina, eighteen years after their first encounter in a train in vienna. they live in paris but they are spending summer vacation in greece in the house of jesse's friends. in the end of the vacation, jesse takes his fourteen year-old son hank to the airport to fly to chicago, where he lives with his mother. one of his greek friends offers a hotel room for jesse and céline to spend one farewell dream night, but they discuss their relationship instead.        written by  claudio carvalho, rio de janeiro, brazil we meet jesse and celine nine years on in greece. almost two decades have passed since their first meeting on that train bound for vienna.     written by  production synopsis
