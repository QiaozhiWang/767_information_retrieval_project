plot summaries 14-year-old arrietty and the rest of the clock family live in peaceful anonymity as they make their own home from items that they borrow from the house's human inhabitants. however, life changes for the clocks when a human boy discovers arrietty.        written by  ann in tokyo, the boy sho has a heart condition and is neglected by his parents. he moves to the house of his aunt sadako and her housemaid haru to wait for a heart surgery, but he is depressed with the absence of his mother. meanwhile the fourteen-year-old arrietty lives on the underground of sadako's house with her father pod and her hysterical mother homily. they are borrowers, tiny people that collect simple things they need in the house. sho sees arrietty in the garden and during the night, she participates for the first time in a harvest with her father. arrietty accidentally drops a sugar cube in sho's bedroom, but the next morning sho leaves the cube near a window in the basement. arrietty's parents decide to move to another place since they have been discovered by an inhabitant of the house. but there is the menace of haru who wants to capture the tiny people.        written by  claudio carvalho, rio de janeiro, brazil the clock family are four-inch-tall people who live anonymously in another family's residence, borrowing simple items to make their home. life changes for the clocks when their daughter, arrietty, is discovered.     written by  imdb editors synopsis before surgery for his heart, a young boy named sho is sent to his family's estate to rest by his aunt...
