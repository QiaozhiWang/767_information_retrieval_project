an adaptation of f. scott fitzgerald's long island-set novel, where midwesterner nick carraway is lured into the lavish world of his neighbor, jay gatsby. soon enough, however, carraway will see through the cracks of gatsby's nouveau riche existence, where obsession, madness, and tragedy await.        written by  anonymous new york,1929. bond-seller nick carraway, in a sanitarium for depression and alcoholism, is persuaded by his doctor to write a therapeutic account of what put him there. nick's journal describes how, seven years earlier, he had moved to a tiny house on long island adjoining the sumptuous mansion owned by enigmatic neighbour, the fabulously wealthy jay gatsby. after attending one of gatsby's legendary parties nick is asked by gatsby to arrange a meeting with his cousin, daisy, now married to the brutish and philandering tom buchanan, who was gatsby's true love, prior to war service. as nick complies he comes to see that gatsby, once a poor boy, has recreated himself as a fascinating millionaire purely to win daisy back but the events of a drunken afternoon conspire to bring about an ending which is anything but happy.        written by  don @ minifie-1 a writer and wall street trader, nick, finds himself drawn to the past and lifestyle of his millionaire neighbor, jay gatsby.     synopsis the film starts with a shot of the flashing green
